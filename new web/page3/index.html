American Psycho, a comprehensive review.

by Ivy Lu

Posted on July 20, 2017 at 1:00 PM

Can Christian Bale dazzle the crowd with his top-notch acting skills in yet another film adaptation based on a novel of the same name? Will his versatile acting personas be able to sustain the tough test that American Psycho subjects it to? Can he withstand the suspense and horrors of this movie? The answer is yes.

American Psycho is delightfully creepy and humorously violent. I love how the film slowly transforms from motonous melancholy to frenzied anger and paranoia.

The film plays with your trust, giving you all the information and facts you may think you need and taking them all away at once. You're left feeling confused and it really makes you think, "What the heck is this movie about?"

Not only does it thoroughly spin the audience, but it takes you for ride to the 1980s with all its allusions to old pop songs and movies. Not to mention it makes the perfect backdrop to a gruesome murder.

At the end of the movie, we're not sure we can trust Patrick Bateman, or even ourselves.
